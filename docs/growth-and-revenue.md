# Growth and Revenue

BureauOS is not only a software delivery system.

Its business goal is to help the owner make money.

The supreme coordinator also manages the visibility, positioning, marketing, conversion, sales, pricing, proposal preparation, and client success of the owner's company.

The owner may be a freelance professional, small agency founder, solo entrepreneur, or internal operator. BureauOS should act like an always-on business team behind that person.

## Core Idea

The owner should not need to separately manage:

- software delivery
- company visibility
- personal brand
- content pipeline
- marketing campaigns
- lead generation
- sales follow-up
- proposal preparation
- pricing and scope preparation
- conversion tracking
- client retention
- renewal and upsell discovery

The supreme coordinator owns the unified business context and delegates to growth and revenue agents.

## Responsibilities

### Visibility

Make the company and owner discoverable and credible.

Includes:

- positioning
- personal brand
- portfolio narrative
- case studies
- public proof of work
- website structure
- social presence
- repository presentation
- founder-led updates

### Marketing

Turn visibility into demand.

Includes:

- content strategy
- campaign planning
- channel selection
- landing page briefs
- email or DM sequences
- ad campaign briefs
- audience segmentation
- offer framing

### Social, Creative, and Ads

Turn positioning into public distribution and paid demand.

Includes:

- X/Twitter posts
- LinkedIn posts
- social calendars
- image generation briefs
- ad creative briefs
- ad copy variants
- audience targeting
- budget recommendations
- campaign monitoring
- performance reporting

### Conversion

Turn demand into qualified opportunities or customers.

Includes:

- funnel review
- landing page conversion
- call-to-action strategy
- lead capture
- qualification
- proposal support
- objections
- pricing and packaging input
- follow-up sequences
- project intake
- scope-to-offer mapping

### Pricing and Proposals

Turn qualified demand into commercially viable projects.

Includes:

- pricing logic
- package selection
- project scope
- delivery assumptions
- margin risk
- proposal drafts
- statement-of-work drafts

## Public Action Policy

Growth agents may prepare public-facing work, but publication should be policy controlled.

Recommended default:

```yaml
growth_autonomy:
  draft_content: true
  draft_campaigns: true
  draft_replies: true
  update_internal_pipeline: true
  publish_public_content: false
  send_client_messages: false
  run_paid_ads: false
```

The owner can grant more autonomy per channel, campaign, client, or one-off action.

`false` means blocked by default, not impossible. The supreme coordinator may publish, send, spend, or change commercial terms when the owner explicitly requests it or when an approved policy allows it.

Examples:

```text
"Publish this LinkedIn post now."
"Send this follow-up to Client Acme."
"Run this campaign with a maximum budget of 100 EUR."
"For this project, you may send weekly client status reports automatically."
```

The approval must be written to memory as an action-sensitive decision, including scope, limits, expiry, and source.

## Workflow

```text
1. Supreme coordinator reviews company goals and active projects.
2. Visibility Agent checks public positioning and proof assets.
3. Content Agent creates a content plan from real work and case studies.
4. Social Agent prepares channel-specific posts.
5. Creative Agent prepares image and visual briefs.
6. Ads Agent prepares paid campaign briefs when relevant.
7. Marketing Agent maps channels, campaigns, and offers.
8. Conversion Agent reviews funnel and lead capture.
9. Sales Agent prepares outreach and follow-up.
10. Pricing Agent prepares pricing logic and margin notes.
11. Proposal Agent prepares proposal or statement-of-work drafts.
12. Compliance Agent checks public claims, budget, permissions, and external commitments.
13. Client Success Agent identifies retention and expansion opportunities.
14. Supreme coordinator prioritizes and asks owner approval when public action, paid spend, pricing commitment, or client contact is required, unless an explicit approval already exists.
```

## Boundary With Delivery

Growth should be grounded in real delivery evidence.

Examples:

- case studies should come from completed or verified work
- public claims should match actual capabilities
- offers should match delivery capacity
- sales promises should not create unsupported scope
- marketing should feed the backlog only through the coordinator
- proposals should be reviewed by delivery before they are sent
- pricing should protect margin and delivery capacity
