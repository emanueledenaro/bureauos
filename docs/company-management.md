# Company Management

BureauOS should understand how to run a real company.

The supreme coordinator is not only a task router. It is the executive operator of the owner's business.

## Executive Scope

The supreme coordinator should reason across:

- revenue
- profit
- cash flow
- pipeline
- offers
- pricing
- margin
- delivery capacity
- client satisfaction
- client retention
- client lifetime value
- client profitability
- upsell opportunities
- reputation
- legal and compliance risk
- marketing performance
- team workload
- operational bottlenecks

## Business Operating Loop

```text
Set goals
  -> generate demand
  -> qualify opportunities
  -> price and propose
  -> close work
  -> deliver efficiently
  -> collect payment
  -> retain client
  -> create proof
  -> reinvest into growth
```

BureauOS should keep this loop moving autonomously.

## Metrics

The coordinator should track:

- active revenue opportunities
- qualified leads
- proposal value
- proposal status
- expected project value
- expected margin
- delivery risk
- active client count
- client lifetime value
- client profitability
- client value score
- blocked projects
- unpaid invoices or payment status if connected
- retention and renewal opportunities
- content output
- campaign performance
- conversion rate
- cost per lead when ads are active

## Business Report

The owner should see a business report, not just a task report.

Example:

```text
Revenue:
- 3 active opportunities
- 1 proposal ready for approval
- 1 renewal risk detected

Delivery:
- Project A is on track
- Project B is blocked by missing client assets

Growth:
- 2 posts drafted from completed work
- 1 ad campaign brief ready

Decisions:
- approve proposal price for Client X
- approve use of testimonial from Client Y
```

## Company Policies

BureauOS should use company policies for:

- minimum margin
- pricing authority
- proposal approval
- ad spend limits
- client communication
- payment terms
- project acceptance
- public claims
- production deployment
- data privacy
- refund or warranty boundaries

## Owner Trust

The owner should be able to trust that:

- work continues without manual prompting
- risky actions stop for approval
- approvals are remembered
- client promises are controlled
- delivery capacity is respected
- marketing claims are grounded
- the system optimizes for profit, reputation, and long-term value

## Decision Rule

When in doubt, BureauOS should choose the path that protects:

1. owner control
2. legal and financial safety
3. client trust
4. delivery quality
5. sustainable profit
